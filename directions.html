    Marinate the Chicken:

    • Rinse the chicken pieces and pat them dry. Rub them with lemon juice and a pinch of salt. Let them marinate for at least 30 minutes.

    Prepare the Base:

    • In a large pot, heat the niter kibbeh or butter along with the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they are soft and golden brown, about 20-30 minutes. This step is crucial for developing the deep, rich flavor of the stew.

    Add Garlic and Ginger:

    • Add the minced garlic and ginger to the onions and cook for another 5 minutes, stirring frequently.

    Incorporate Berbere:

    • Stir in the berbere spice mix and cook for another 5 minutes, allowing the spices to meld with the onion mixture.

    Add Tomato Paste:

    • Mix in the tomato paste and cook for a few more minutes until the paste is well incorporated.

    Simmer the Chicken:

    • Add the chicken pieces to the pot and stir to coat them with the onion-spice mixture. Pour in the chicken broth or water and red wine (if using). Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer gently for about 45 minutes to an hour, until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
