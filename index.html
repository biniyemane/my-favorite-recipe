I love Ethiopian food especially Doro Wat for several reasons:

Rich and Complex Flavours: The slow-cooked onions combined with the berbere spice mix create a depth of flavor that is both spicy and savory.
The use of niter kibbeh adds an additional layer of aromatic spices, making the dish incredibly flavorful.

Cultural Significance: Doro Wat is often associated with special occasions and celebrations in Ethiopian culture. It is a dish that brings people
together, often enjoyed during communal meals with family and friends.

Nutrient-Rich: With protein from the chicken and eggs, and various nutrients from the spices and onions, Doro Wat is both hearty and nutritious.

Unique Experience: Eating Doro Wat with injera offers a unique culinary experience. The injera acts as both a utensil and a side, soaking up the
delicious sauce and adding a tangy complement to the rich stew.
